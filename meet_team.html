Faculty Advisors

Dr. Joseph C. Majdalani, Ph.D., P.E.

Dr. Majdalani received his Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Utah in 1995. He currently serves as the Department Chair of Aerospace Engineering at Auburn University and as an Auburn Alumni Engineering Council Endowed Professor. Dr. Majdalani's research devotes itself to the theoretical and computational modeling of rocket propulsion systems that are mainly driven by either wall-normal or wall-tangential injection. Recently, his work on compressible gas motions has required the development of a systematic procedure for modeling high speed flow problems. Thus, with such a rich history in analyses of rocket systems, Dr. Majdalani serves as the perfect advisor for the Auburn Student Launch Team.

Dr. Eldon Triggs, Ph.D.

Dr. Triggs currently works as an instructor in the Aerospace Engineering Department at Auburn University. He teaches the Aerodynamics III: Boundary Layer Theory and Senior Launch Vehicle Design courses. Dr. Triggs is also responsible for the operation of the fabrication and advanced aerosystems laboratories. His extensive experience in composites and high powered rocketry greatly augments the design process for Auburn's Student Launch Team. Having completed numerous Level 2 launches, and being based in the college itself Dr. Triggs serves as an ideal candidate for the team's tripoli mentor.

Benjamin Bauldree

Benjamin Bauldree is a returning member of the Auburn University USLI 2013-2014 team. Benjamin was the Vehicle Design team lead for that years team, helping them reach the final leg of the competition and ultimatley successfully launching their rocket at the Bonneville Salt Flats. After graduating in 2014 with his BS in Aerospace Engineering, Benjamin began working for the U.S. Air Force out of Eglin AFB as a Weapons Test Engineer for the 780th Test Squadron. He has since started the Masters of Aerospace Engineering Program at Auburn University, and has taken on the role as graduate advisor to this years team. His past experience with rocketry and his background in testing will make him a beneficial part of the team.
